# Meta – Hiring Process for Tech Roles

## Company Overview
Meta Platforms Inc. (formerly Facebook) is an American multinational technology company headquartered in Menlo Park, California.
Meta owns and operates Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp, Threads, and is heavily investing in AI, AR/VR through Meta Quest and Reality Labs.

- **Founded:** 2004 (as Facebook), rebranded to Meta in 2021
- **Headquarters:** Menlo Park, California, USA
- **CEO:** Mark Zuckerberg
- **India Offices:** Gurugram (primary), Mumbai
- **Products:** Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp, Threads, Meta Quest (VR), Reality Labs, Llama AI
- **Employees:** Nearly 72,000 globally
- **Scale:** Meta connects billions of people globally. Receives over 250,000 applications every quarter. Only approximately 1–2% of candidates successfully navigate the entire interview process.
- **Acceptance Rate (SWE):** Average of 27 days from application to hire. Approximately 90% of candidates do not make it past resume screening.

## Meta Engineering Levels

Meta uses an E-level system for engineers:

- **E3** – New Grad / Junior Engineer (0–2 years)
- **E4** – Software Engineer (2–5 years)
- **E5** – Senior Software Engineer (5–8 years)
- **E6** – Staff Software Engineer (8–12 years)
- **E7** – Senior Staff Software Engineer
- **E8** – Principal Engineer
- **E9** – Distinguished Engineer (extremely rare)

E5 is considered the critical career level at Meta. Behavioral round performance alone can decide whether you are leveled at E4 or E5 — making behavioral preparation just as important as technical.

## Hiring Tracks

### 1. University / New Grad Hiring (E3)
For final year students or recent graduates.
Focus is on DSA coding rounds — 2 coding rounds in the onsite loop.
System design is either not asked or at a significantly lower bar.
Process: OA + Phone Screen + Onsite Loop (2 coding + 1 behavioral).

### 2. Experienced / Lateral Hiring (E4 and above)
For candidates with 2+ years of industry experience.
Full onsite loop: 1 coding + 1 AI-enabled coding + 1 system design or product architecture + 1 behavioral.
E5 and above: Deeper system design evaluation and leadership assessment.

### 3. E6 Hiring (Staff Engineer)
Slightly different process — includes a Leadership Assessment interview after the phone screen and before the onsite.
Onsite for E6: 1 standard coding + 1 AI-enabled coding + 1 architecture round + 1 design round + 1 behavioral.

### 4. Internship Hiring
Standard internship process: OA + 1–2 technical rounds.
Strong conversion rate to full-time E3 offers.
Meta internships are highly competitive and well-paid.

## Step-by-Step Hiring Process (2025–2026 Updated)

### Step 1 – Application and Resume Screening
Apply via metacareers.com or through a referral.
Referrals significantly improve shortlisting chances — a friend at Meta can refer you directly to the recruiter.
Resume screening is the most competitive step — approximately 90% of candidates are filtered here.
Resume tips: highlight measurable impact, large-scale systems experience, relevant projects.
Recruiters look at: companies you have worked at, scale of systems you have built, technical depth.

### Step 2 – Recruiter Call
Informal phone or video call with a Meta recruiter (15–30 minutes).
Nothing technical — recruiter builds a picture of your background, motivation, and trajectory.
They explain the interview process in detail including rounds and what to expect.
Real experience (Rohit Verma, E5 offer): recruiter walked through the full process in detail — 1 screening round and 4 final interview rounds.

### Step 3 – Online Assessment (OA) [NEW addition in 2025]
Meta introduced the OA as a new first screening step in 2025.
Administered through CodeSignal with full video and microphone monitoring throughout the 90-minute session.
You must keep your video and mic on for the entire duration — it is proctored.
Must pass this before moving to the phone screen stage.
Difficulty: Medium LeetCode level.
Not every candidate gets an OA — some industry hires go straight to phone screen.

### Step 4 – Technical Phone Screen
One or two 45-minute sessions over video call.
Format: a few behavioral questions at the start, then most time spent on coding questions.
Questions: Meta-tagged LeetCode easy to medium problems.
Code in a plain text editor — no syntax highlighting, no autocomplete.
Tip: Practice coding without any IDE assistance — Meta uses bare-bones editors throughout.

### Step 5 – Full Onsite Loop (Main Round)
The most critical stage. Typically 4 rounds for E4/E5, 5 rounds for E6.
Each round is 45 minutes.

#### Round 1 – Traditional Coding Round (All Levels)
Standard LeetCode-style DSA problems.
Difficulty: Medium to Hard.
Most frequently asked topics: Arrays, Strings, Trees, Graphs, Dynamic Programming, Two Pointers.
Meta evaluates: efficiency, structure, syntax, bugs, and working code.
You do NOT need to write perfect production-quality code — but it should work.
You might fail to finish a coding problem due to time constraints and still get an offer — communication matters more.
Real experience (Glassdoor, Sep 2025): "Standard leetcode medium with follow-ups on edge cases and optimizations."
Key tip from ex-Meta SWE: "Make sure you are not trying to boil the ocean or impress by solving in isolation — communicate your ideas."

#### Round 2 – AI-Enabled Coding Round [NEW in 2025]
Meta began piloting this round in October 2025 — in pilot mode as of Q4 2025 so not every candidate gets it yet.
Confirmed in official Meta Careers hiring process page and Glassdoor reports (Sep 2025, London).
In this round you have access to an authorized AI assistant within CoderPad during the interview.
Questions, focus areas, and evaluation criteria are enhanced to reflect current coding skills like debugging and code review.
The round tests how well you work alongside AI — not just raw algorithmic thinking.
Focus: debugging, code review, using AI to enhance your solution, not replace your thinking.
Treat it like a working session — not a silent test.

#### Round 3 – System Design OR Product Architecture Round
The type of round depends on the role you are applying for:
- "Software Engineer, Infrastructure" roles get a System Design interview.
- "Software Engineer, Product" roles get a Product Architecture interview.

System Design interviews focus on: distributed systems, scalability, performance, and efficiency.
Product Architecture interviews focus on: API design, client-server interactions, usability, and how products evolve over time.
Both are evaluated on: Problem navigation, solution design, technical excellence, technical communication.
Common system design questions at Meta: Design Facebook News Feed, Design Instagram, Design WhatsApp messaging, Design a URL shortener, Design a real-time notification system, Design a video streaming service like Instagram Reels, Design a distributed cache.
For E5+: Much deeper evaluation — explain trade-offs, scalability decisions, and production-level thinking.

#### Round 4 – Behavioral Round (Jedi Interview)
Meta calls this the "Jedi Interview" internally.
Behavioral questions appear in every round — but this round is dedicated entirely to them.
Meta evaluates candidates against its core values: Move Fast, Build Awesome Things, Focus on Long-Term Impact, Be Open, Be Bold, and Build Social Value.
Engineering-first culture: Meta was founded and led by engineers — they value bias for action and aggressive execution.
Use STAR method: Situation, Task, Action, Result — but be specific and show measurable impact.
Meta values authenticity — interviewers want honest reflections on failures and growth, not polished perfect stories.
For E5: Behavioral round performance alone can result in being downleveled from E5 to E4 — take it seriously.
For E6: A separate Leadership Assessment round is added before the onsite to evaluate leadership at staff level.

### Step 6 – Candidate Review Meeting
After your onsite, all interviewers file feedback and grades into the system.
A candidate review meeting is held — all interviewers are invited.
They usually only attend if there is strong disagreement (e.g., 2 no-hires and 2 hires).
A hire / no-hire recommendation is made. If the team still cannot agree, you may be asked to do a follow-up interview.

### Step 7 – Hiring Committee
Includes senior leaders from across Meta.
Usually follows the recommendation of the candidate review meeting — more of a formality.
Main focus: fine-tuning the exact level (E4 vs E5) and compensation you will be offered.

### Step 8 – Team Matching
After hiring committee approval, you enter team matching phase.
1–2 sessions of 45 minutes with potential teams.
These sessions start with behavioral questions and then discuss your interest in the team's work.
You can express team preferences — Meta tries to match you with a team that fits your skills and interests.

### Step 9 – Offer and Negotiation
Offer includes: Base Salary + Performance Bonus (yearly) + RSUs (4-year vesting) + Signing Bonus.
Negotiation is expected — always negotiate, especially on RSUs and signing bonus.
Tip from Rohit Verma (E5, London): Research optimal compensation before the call, prepare a counteroffer, disclose competing offers — Meta's recruiter was able to advocate for a better offer within 2 days.

## Behavioral Preparation — Meta Culture and Values

Meta's core values that are assessed in the Jedi Interview:
- **Move Fast** — bias for action, push through barriers aggressively, ship quickly
- **Build Awesome Things** — focus on building impactful products
- **Focus on Long-Term Impact** — think beyond the immediate feature
- **Be Open** — straightforward communication, willingness to have hard conversations
- **Be Bold** — take risks, make big bets
- **Build Social Value** — genuine belief in connecting people and communities

### What Meta Culture Looks Like in Practice
Meta has an engineering-first culture — founded and led by engineers.
Flat organization — preference for consensus-building over top-down decision making.
"Move Fast" no longer includes "Break Things" but still means pushing through barriers aggressively.
Comfort with ambiguity — ability to make progress without perfect information.
Diverse viewpoints and authentic communication are valued.

### Common Behavioral Questions at Meta
- Tell me about a time you moved fast and what happened.
- Describe a project where you had significant impact. How did you measure it?
- Tell me about a time you had a conflict with a teammate. How did you resolve it?
- Give an example of when you took initiative without being asked.
- Tell me about a time you failed. What did you learn?
- Describe a situation where you had to make a decision with incomplete information.
- Tell me about a time you disagreed with your manager and what you did.
- Give an example of a project where you demonstrated long-term thinking.

Prepare 6–8 strong STAR stories. Align each story to Meta's core values. Meta values specificity and measurable impact — quantify your results whenever possible.

## Tips to Crack Meta (2025 Updated)

- Practice LeetCode Medium to Hard — use Meta company tag and focus on the most frequent topics.
- Always practice coding in a plain text editor — no autocomplete, no syntax highlighting. This is how Meta interviews work.
- Build on top of your solutions — Meta interviewers add follow-up optimizations. Proactively think about edge cases and optimizations yourself.
- For the AI-enabled coding round: treat it like a working session, not a silent test. Use the AI tool effectively to debug and improve, not replace your thinking.
- Study Meta's real systems — read the Meta Engineering Blog. Understanding how News Feed, Messenger, and Instagram work gives massive advantage in system design.
- Prepare 6–8 strong behavioral STAR stories aligned to Meta's core values: Move Fast, Impact, Openness, Boldness.
- Quantify your impact in behavioral stories — Meta values measurable results.
- Be authentic — honest reflections on failures and growth resonate more than polished perfect stories.
- For E5 targeting: prepare behavioral stories as seriously as technical prep — behavioral alone can downlevel you from E5 to E4.
- Get a referral if possible — it dramatically improves shortlisting chances.
- Research compensation on Levels.fyi and Blind before your offer call — always prepare a counteroffer.
- Disclose competing offers when negotiating — Meta recruiters can advocate for better packages when they know you have alternatives.
- Make interviews conversational — check in with your interviewer: "Does that context make sense?" or "Would you like me to go deeper?"

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What is the Jedi Interview at Meta?**
A: The Jedi Interview is Meta's internal name for the behavioral interview round. It evaluates cultural fit and alignment with Meta's core values: Move Fast, Build Awesome Things, Focus on Long-Term Impact, Be Open, Be Bold. Despite the name it is not a casual round — for E5 candidates it can determine your level or result in rejection even with strong technical performance.

**Q: What is the AI-enabled coding round at Meta?**
A: Introduced in October 2025 as a pilot. In this round you have access to authorized AI tools within CoderPad during the interview. It tests how well you work alongside AI — debugging, code review, and using AI to enhance your solution. Not every candidate gets it yet as it is still being rolled out.

**Q: Is system design asked for new grads at Meta?**
A: Generally no for E3. New grads typically get 2 coding rounds and 1 behavioral. System design is added from E4 onwards and becomes progressively more important at E5 and above.

**Q: Can behavioral performance really get me downleveled at Meta?**
A: Yes. For E5 candidates, behavioral round performance alone can result in being offered E4 instead of E5. Meta explicitly states this — leadership and impact demonstrated in behavioral rounds significantly influence the leveling decision.

**Q: What is the difference between System Design and Product Architecture rounds at Meta?**
A: It depends on the role. Infrastructure SWE roles get System Design (distributed systems, scalability, performance). Product SWE roles get Product Architecture (API design, client-server interactions, product evolution). Both are open-ended conversations and evaluated on the same criteria: problem navigation, solution design, technical excellence, and communication.

**Q: Does Meta hire from India?**
A: Yes. Meta has offices in Gurugram and Mumbai. However, Meta India hiring is significantly more selective than their US hiring. Most India roles are E4 and above — very few new grad positions are available in India directly.

**Q: Can I reapply after rejection at Meta?**
A: Yes. Meta typically allows reapplication after 6–12 months. Use that time to strengthen weak areas identified during your previous attempt.

**Q: How important are referrals at Meta?**
A: Very important — referrals from current Meta employees directly improve shortlisting chances and expedite the process. The recruiter contacted Rohit Verma within one week of his referral being submitted.
