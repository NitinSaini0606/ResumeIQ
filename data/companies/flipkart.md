# Flipkart – Hiring Process for Tech Roles

## Company Overview
Flipkart is India's largest e-commerce marketplace, founded in 2007 by Sachin Bansal and Binny Bansal.
Flipkart is owned by Walmart (acquired in 2018) and operates as India's leading online shopping platform competing with Amazon India.

- **Founded:** 2007
- **Headquarters:** Bengaluru, Karnataka, India
- **Parent Company:** Walmart Inc.
- **CEO:** Kalyan Krishnamurthy
- **Office:** Bengaluru (primary HQ), with teams across India
- **Products:** E-commerce marketplace, Flipkart Pay, Flipkart Health, Myntra, Cleartrip, Shopsy
- **Scale:** Registered customer base of over 100 million. Offers 80+ million products across 80+ categories.
- **Interview Difficulty:** Rated 3.2 out of 5 on Glassdoor. 64% of candidates rate their experience as positive.

## Flipkart Engineering Levels

Flipkart uses SDE levels internally:

- **SDE 1** – Entry level / New Grad (0–2 years)
- **SDE 2** – Mid level (2–5 years)
- **SDE 3** – Senior Engineer (5–8 years)
- **SDE 4** – Staff / Principal level (8+ years)
- **Senior Architect** – Very senior, niche roles

## Hiring Tracks

### 1. Campus / New Grad Hiring (SDE 1)
For final year students or recent graduates with up to 2 years of experience.
On-campus recruitment or through Flipkart GRiD competition (see hackathon section).
Process: OA + Machine Coding + DSA rounds + Hiring Manager round.

### 2. Experienced / Lateral Hiring (SDE 2 and above)
For candidates with 2+ years of industry experience.
System Design round is added for SDE 2 and above.
SDE 3+ includes deeper HLD discussions and project deep-dives.

### 3. Off-Campus Hiring
Flipkart also runs off-campus drives for candidates with 0.6 to 2 years of experience.
Posted on LinkedIn, Naukri, and Flipkart Careers portal.
Minimum requirement: Strong understanding of DSA and time/space complexity.

## Flipkart GRiD — Flagship Hiring Hackathon (Most Important for Students)

Flipkart GRiD is India's most prestigious national-level engineering campus competition.
It is Flipkart's primary route for hiring top student talent across India.
GRiD 7.0 was held in 2025 — the 7th edition of this competition.

### What is Flipkart GRiD?
GRiD is Flipkart's flagship engineering campus challenge where students compete on real-world e-commerce problems and coding challenges inspired by Flipkart's actual systems.
GRiD 5.0 (2023) received 4,76,418 registrations — surpassing CAT 2022 in participation. It is India's Number 1 Flagship Engineering Campus Competition.

### GRiD 7.0 (2025) Key Details
- Registration opened: June 16, 2025
- Registration deadline: June 30, 2025 (extended to July 6, 2025)
- Grand Finale: On-site at Flipkart HQ, Bengaluru
- Participation: Completely FREE for all eligible students
- Prizes: ₹6 Lakhs worth of prizes
- Tracks: Software Development, Information Security, Robotics, Health

### GRiD Eligibility
- Open to students pursuing B.Tech, B.E., M.Tech, M.S., or related Engineering programs
- Students from ALL engineering colleges in India (and USA)
- ALL engineering disciplines are eligible — not just CS
- Team size: 1 to 3 members, only from the same college
- Must be a full-time student and Indian citizen
- Must not have participated in any other Flipkart hackathon in the past 3 months

### GRiD Competition Rounds
1. **Screening Round** — Shortlisting based on academic profile, grades, projects, and achievements. No coding test in this round.
2. **Coding Assessment** — 90-minute online test with 3 coding problems (C, C++, Java, or Python). Medium difficulty.
3. **Case Study Round** — Solve a real Flipkart business problem by building a tool or system and submitting code.
4. **National Finals** — Top 48 teams visit Flipkart HQ Bengaluru, form teams, build a prototype, and present a live demo to senior Flipkart tech and product leaders. Evaluated on innovation, execution, technical strength, and business relevance.

### GRiD Rewards and Hiring Outcomes
- Prize pool: ₹6 Lakhs in prizes
- Top 50 participants featured by Flipkart
- Finalists receive Letters of Recommendation
- 3rd year students: Summer Internship interviews for SDE role (Stipend: ₹1,00,000 per month)
- 4th year students: Full-time SDE 1 role offers or Winter Internship (SDE 1 Compensation: ₹32 LPA)
- Final selection based on Case Study, National Finals performance, and interview evaluation by Flipkart

### How to Apply for GRiD
Apply via the MyCareernet portal — provide academic and personal details.
Keep checking Flipkart's official channels for GRiD announcement every year (typically June–July).
Also available on Unstop platform.

## Step-by-Step Hiring Process (2025 Updated)

### Step 1 – Application
Apply via Flipkart Careers: flipkart.com/careers or through job portals like LinkedIn and Naukri.
Referrals from current Flipkart employees help significantly.
For campus hiring, Flipkart visits colleges directly or candidates apply through GRiD competition.
Real experience (Anmol Mittal, SDE 2 offer, 2024): Applied randomly in January 2024, got no response. Received recruiter email in March about a drive. Was initially ghosted — received same mail again 1 month later and this time the round got scheduled.

### Step 2 – Online Assessment (OA)
Conducted on HackerRank or similar platforms.
Duration: 90 minutes.
Format: 2–3 coding problems covering DSA.
Difficulty: Medium level.
Real experience (Glassdoor, Aug 2025): Applied through Flipkart GRiD. Received OA link after many days. OA was medium difficulty and manageable within time.
Focus areas: Data Structures, Algorithms, problem-solving efficiency.

### Step 3 – Machine Coding Round (Unique to Flipkart — Very Important)
This is the most unique and important round at Flipkart — not common at other companies.
Duration: 60–90 minutes.
Format: Build a complete working mini-application or system from scratch.
This is a live coding round — you write production-ready, working code.
Real question (Glassdoor, Aug 2025): Implement a URL shortener.
Real question (SDE 2, 2024): Machine coding round was first round — new to the candidate but manageable.
What is evaluated: Clean code architecture, OOP principles, working functionality, code quality, modularity.
This round is an elimination round — candidates who write messy or non-working code are filtered out.
Tips: Practice building small working systems from scratch — parking lot, library management, URL shortener, snake game, tic-tac-toe.

### Step 4 – DSA Rounds (Problem Solving)
Usually 2 rounds of pure DSA problem-solving.
Duration: 45–60 minutes each.
Format: 1–2 LeetCode-style problems per round, solved live on CoderPad.
Difficulty: Medium to Hard.
Real experience (Glassdoor, Aug 2025): Round 1 had 2 medium LeetCode problems. Round 2 had 1 medium and 1 hard.
Real experience (Glassdoor, 2025): DSA rounds included min-heap and max-heap questions.
Interviewers ask follow-up questions and explore alternate solutions or optimizations.
This is also an elimination round.

### Step 5 – System Design Round (SDE 2 and above)
Added for mid-level and senior roles.
Duration: 60 minutes.
Format: Design a scalable system relevant to e-commerce.
Common questions: Design a URL shortener, Design a search system, Design a flash sale system, Design Flipkart's product catalog, Design a payment processing system, Design a delivery tracking system.
For SDE 2: Basic HLD expected. Scalability and trade-offs are discussed.
For SDE 3+: Deep HLD with detailed discussion on bottlenecks, data modeling, and production considerations.
Real experience (SDE 2, 2024): System design was scheduled but interviewer ran out of time in machine coding round — skipped to next round. Shows process is flexible and team-dependent.

### Step 6 – Hiring Manager Round
Conversational round focused on cultural fit, team alignment, and career goals.
Duration: 45–60 minutes.
Format: Resume walkthrough, project deep-dives, behavioral questions, career goal discussion.
Real experience (SDE 2, 2024): First HM round was with a Director of Engineering (DOE). Asked about academic background, CGPA, high school interests, and project questions. Candidate did not receive callback for 3 days — then got call saying there were callouts and the round was rescheduled. This time with a Senior Engineering Manager who was more conversational.
Flipkart values: Cultural fit, mentoring ability, long-term alignment with Flipkart's engineering goals.
Tips: Know your resume projects inside out. Be prepared to explain architecture, challenges, and your specific contributions.

### Step 7 – HR Round
Final round focused on behavioral questions, work ethic, motivations, and offer discussion.
Duration: 30 minutes.
Questions: Why Flipkart, salary expectations, notice period, relocation.
Flipkart values cultural fit — highlight instances where you contributed to a project's success or mentored someone.

## Tips to Crack Flipkart (2025)

- Practice Machine Coding SPECIFICALLY — this is Flipkart's most unique and important round. Build URL shorteners, parking lot systems, library managers, ride-sharing systems from scratch.
- Focus on clean code in machine coding — production-quality, modular, well-named variables. Bad code quality fails candidates even if logic is correct.
- Practice LeetCode Medium and Hard — heap, graph, and DP problems are frequently reported.
- For system design: focus on e-commerce problems — flash sales, catalog search, payment systems.
- Know your resume projects thoroughly — Flipkart HM rounds heavily discuss your past work.
- Apply through GRiD if you are a student — it is the fastest track to a Flipkart offer with cash prizes and LoR as added benefits.
- Be patient with the process — response times can be slow. Follow up with recruiters politely.
- Prepare for team-specific interviews — different Flipkart teams run slightly different processes.
- Highlight experience with high-traffic systems — Flipkart values engineers who understand India-scale engineering.

Key tip: DSA with graphs and heaps is very commonly reported at Flipkart. Prepare these thoroughly.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is Flipkart good for freshers in India?**
A: Yes. Flipkart is one of India's top product companies and excellent for building a strong engineering foundation. SDE 1 offers are competitive at ₹24–32 LPA and the engineering challenges at Flipkart's scale are significant.

**Q: What is the Machine Coding round at Flipkart?**
A: A unique Flipkart round where you build a complete working mini-application from scratch in 60–90 minutes. Unlike DSA rounds, you write complete working code with clean architecture. Practice building systems like URL shorteners, parking lots, and ride-sharing systems.

**Q: Does Flipkart hire from non-IIT colleges?**
A: Yes. Flipkart hires from all colleges in India — the GRiD competition is specifically open to ALL engineering colleges regardless of tier.

**Q: What is Flipkart GRiD?**
A: India's most prestigious national engineering campus competition organized by Flipkart. GRiD offers cash prizes up to ₹6 Lakhs, Letters of Recommendation, and direct hiring into SDE 1 roles or internships. It is the best route for students to get into Flipkart.

**Q: Does Flipkart offer remote work?**
A: Flipkart follows a hybrid work culture. Most roles are Bengaluru-based.

**Q: Can I reapply after rejection?**
A: Yes. Flipkart allows reapplication after a cooling-off period. Use that time to strengthen DSA and machine coding skills.
