# Amazon – Hiring Process for Tech Roles

## Company Overview
Amazon.com, Inc. is an American multinational technology and e-commerce conglomerate founded in 1994 by Jeff Bezos. It is one of the "Big Five" US tech companies alongside Google, Microsoft, Apple, and Meta. Amazon has transformed from an online bookstore into one of the world's most powerful tech companies, operating across e-commerce, cloud computing (AWS), digital streaming (Prime Video), artificial intelligence, and logistics.

- **Founded:** 1994
- **Headquarters:** Seattle, Washington, USA
- **CEO:** Andy Jassy (since 2021)
- **India Offices:** Bengaluru, Hyderabad, Chennai, Pune, Mumbai, Gurugram, Noida
- **Products/Services:** Amazon.com, AWS (Amazon Web Services), Prime Video, Alexa, Kindle, Ring, Twitch, Whole Foods
- **Scale:** Amazon employs over 1.5 million people worldwide. AWS alone powers millions of companies globally. Amazon processes over 1.6 million shipments per day.
- **Return to Office Policy (2025):** Starting 2025, Amazon has mandated a strict return-to-office policy under CEO Andy Jassy, requiring employees to be in-office 5 days a week. Remote workers were required to relocate closer to a designated office hub within two months or face potential termination.

## Amazon's 16 Leadership Principles (LPs) — The Core of Every Interview

Amazon evaluates every candidate against its 16 Leadership Principles. **This is the single most important thing to prepare for Amazon.** Unlike Google or Meta, Amazon places far greater emphasis on behavioral alignment than any other FAANG company. Every interviewer is assigned 1–3 LPs to assess you on.

1. **Customer Obsession** – Leaders start with the customer and work backwards. The north star of all 16 LPs.
2. **Ownership** – Leaders act on behalf of the entire company. Never say "that's not my job."
3. **Invent and Simplify** – Leaders seek innovation and simplicity; always open to ideas from everywhere.
4. **Are Right, A Lot** – Strong judgment, good decisions, seek diverse perspectives.
5. **Learn and Be Curious** – Lifelong learners, constantly exploring new possibilities.
6. **Hire and Develop the Best** – Raise the performance bar with every hire; develop leaders.
7. **Insist on the Highest Standards** – Relentlessly high standards; problems are fixed so they stay fixed.
8. **Think Big** – Think differently, look around corners, inspire results.
9. **Bias for Action** – Speed matters; calculated risk-taking is valued.
10. **Frugality** – Accomplish more with less; constraints breed resourcefulness.
11. **Earn Trust** – Listen attentively, speak candidly, treat others respectfully.
12. **Dive Deep** – Stay connected to details; audit frequently.
13. **Have Backbone; Disagree and Commit** – Challenge decisions respectfully, commit fully once a decision is made.
14. **Deliver Results** – Focus on key inputs, deliver with the right quality and in a timely fashion.
15. **Strive to Be Earth's Best Employer** – Leaders lead with empathy, create a safe and productive environment.
16. **Success and Scale Bring Broad Responsibility** – Be humble, be careful about secondary effects of actions on the world.

> **Pro Tip from real Amazon interviewers:** Don't try to guess which specific LP each question maps to. Instead, make every answer signal "Customer Obsession" — it is the north star that connects all 16 principles. Focus on "I" not "We" in your answers; Amazon wants to know your individual contribution.

## Step-by-Step Hiring Process (2025–2026 Updated)

### Overview
The overall process has **7 key stages** and takes **4–8 weeks** on average (though SDE intern hiring can take up to 59 days based on Glassdoor data).

---

### Step 1 – Application
- Apply via **amazon.jobs** (official portal), LinkedIn, or through employee referrals.
- Filter by role (Internships, Full-Time), country, and function.
- Amazon also actively recruits at IITs, NITs, IIITs, and select Tier-2 colleges in India.
- Resume tips: Tailor your resume to the job description. Use the STAR format even in bullet points. Include measurable impact — "reduced latency by 40%" beats "improved system performance."
- Amazon uses ATS scanning — include relevant keywords from the JD.
- Resume template available on amazon.jobs.
- **Referrals** from current Amazonian employees improve shortlisting chances significantly.
- For freshers: highlight GitHub projects, competitive programming rankings, hackathon wins, and internships.

---

### Step 2 – Recruiter Screen
- If resume is shortlisted, a recruiter calls (15–45 minutes).
- Topics covered: your background, preferred role, location, availability, and timeline.
- Recruiter will outline what rounds to expect for your specific role/level.
- Build a rapport — recruiters coordinate all scheduling and can provide useful tips.

---

### Step 3 – Online Assessment (OA)
- Conducted on Amazon's internal platform (HireVue) or HackerRank/HirePro (campus hires).
- **Format:** Usually 2 DSA coding problems + 1 Work Simulation / Behavioral section (20 questions aligned to Leadership Principles).
- **Duration:** 60–90 minutes total.
- **Difficulty:** Easy to Medium LeetCode equivalent (Medium for experienced roles).
- The behavioral section is taken very seriously — be careful and consistent. Amazon applies these answers to their LP framework.
- Real experience (SDE Intern, Mar 2026, Bengaluru, Glassdoor): "DSA section included a partition dynamic programming problem. Behavioral section forced application of LP principles to simulated work scenarios."
- Real experience (SDE OA, Feb 2026, 1Point3Acres): "Amazon SWE II Online Assessment — 2 coding problems, standard LP behavioral questions."
- Not always present for experienced hires referred by employees.

---

### Step 4 – Technical Phone Screen
- 1 round with an Amazon engineer (30–45 minutes) over Amazon Chime or phone.
- 1–2 DSA coding problems (Easy to Medium difficulty).
- Behavioral questions woven in — expect 1–2 LP-based questions even here.
- Coding done verbally or on a shared online IDE/document.
- After clearing: recruiter schedules the interview loop.

---

### Step 5 – Virtual Onsite / Interview Loop (Main Round)

This is the most critical stage. Typically **5–7 rounds** for experienced hires and **3–4 rounds** for new grads. Each round is 45–60 minutes. As of 2025, some roles are reintroducing at least one in-person round.

You will meet with **2–7 interviewers** including engineers, managers, and a **Bar Raiser**.

#### What is the Bar Raiser?
The Bar Raiser is a specially trained Amazon interviewer from **outside your immediate team**. Their job is to ensure every new hire raises the bar for talent at Amazon. They have **veto power over hiring decisions**. You will not always know who the Bar Raiser is — treat every interviewer as if they are the Bar Raiser.

---

#### Round 1 & 2 – Coding / DSA Rounds (All Levels)
- 1–2 DSA problems per round with follow-up questions asking for optimization.
- Difficulty: Easy to Medium (sometimes Hard for senior roles).
- Coding on shared IDE (Amazon Chime or CoderPad).
- Amazon often asks **"practical" coding questions** — data structures for real-world systems like shopping carts, LRU caches, inventory systems — more so than pure algorithmic puzzles.
- Interviewers evaluate: correctness, edge case handling, code readability, time/space complexity, communication.
- Real experience (SDE Intern Onsite, 2025, 1Point3Acres): "Morse Code and Word Break II asked in virtual onsite."
- Real experience (SDE I, Apr 2026, Glassdoor): "Unconventional coding problem — required creative thinking rather than standard DSA patterns."
- Real experience (Medium, 2025): "Amazon asks LeetCode medium-level questions — LRU Cache was reported as the most common DSA question asked."

**Most Common DSA Questions Reported (2025–2026):**
- LRU Cache (very frequently reported — #1 most common)
- Find Median from Data Stream
- Search in a Rotated Sorted Array
- Longest Substring Without Repeating Characters
- Peak Index in a Mountain Array
- Word Break II
- Partition DP problems

---

#### Round 3 – System Design Round (SDE II and Above)
- **Not required for SDE I** (new grads get an additional coding round or LP round instead).
- Added from SDE II (L5) onwards.
- Duration: 45–60 minutes.
- Format: open-ended — "Design Amazon's recommendation system", "Design a parking lot system", etc.
- Unlike Google, Amazon system design questions often directly reference Amazon products.
- Interviewers expect you to apply Leadership Principles in your design (Customer Obsession, Frugality, etc.).
- You are expected to interact with the interviewer like a customer — ask clarifying questions, identify vague requirements.

**Most Common System Design Questions Asked at Amazon (Confirmed 2025–2026):**
- Design a parking lot system (very frequently reported — confirmed 2025/2026)
- Design Amazon Prime Video / Netflix (video streaming at scale)
- Design Amazon's product recommendation system
- Design Amazon S3 (distributed file storage)
- Design a payment gateway / Stripe-like system
- Design Uber / ride-hailing system
- Design a distributed messaging system (like SQS)
- Design a URL shortener
- Design a web crawler
- Design a real-time leaderboard / coding challenge platform (like LeetCode)
- Design a CDN architecture
- Design an e-commerce order management system
- Design Amazon's product search / autocomplete

**What Interviewers Look For in System Design:**
- Clarify functional and non-functional requirements FIRST — don't jump into design
- Outline High-Level Design (HLD) before Low-Level Design (LLD)
- Mention trade-offs clearly — e.g., "DynamoDB for scale but Aurora if we need strong consistency"
- Amazon-specific patterns to mention: microservices ("two-pizza team" model), event-driven architecture (SQS/Kinesis), CQRS (separate read/write paths), AWS-native services
- Think at Amazon scale — millions of concurrent users, global availability
- Connect design decisions back to Leadership Principles (Customer Obsession, Frugality, etc.)

---

#### Round 4 – Behavioral / Leadership Principles Round
- Every round has LP questions woven in, but this round focuses entirely on them.
- Use the STAR format: Situation, Task, Action, Result.
- Amazon wants specifics with data: "I reduced latency by 40%" not "I improved performance."
- Focus on "I" not "We" — interviewers want to know your individual contribution.
- Prepare 4–5 strong STAR stories covering: conflict resolution, ownership of failures, leadership without authority, handling ambiguity, influencing decisions, delivering results under pressure.

**Common LP Questions Asked:**
- Tell me about a time you disagreed with your manager or team.
- Describe a project where you showed leadership without formal authority.
- Tell me about a time you failed and what you learned.
- How do you handle working under ambiguity?
- Tell me about a time you had to deal with a very difficult customer.
- Describe a tough situation where you had to step into a leadership role.
- Tell me about a time you solved a complex problem and how you went about it.
- Tell me about a time you convinced team members of something you proposed.

---

#### Round 5–7 – Additional Rounds (Bar Raiser / Domain-Specific)
- Senior roles (SDE II+) often have 1–2 additional rounds.
- Bar Raiser round: technical + behavioral, can be any format.
- Domain-specific rounds for ML, data engineering, infrastructure, mobile, etc.
- For SDM (managers): additional "people management" behavioral rounds.

---

### Step 6 – Interviewer Debrief / Hiring Committee
- All interviewers meet (debrief) to discuss feedback.
- Unlike Google's separate Hiring Committee, at Amazon the hiring decision is reached through the debrief + Bar Raiser's input.
- The Bar Raiser has **veto power** — if they flag a no-hire, the candidate is typically rejected even if all other interviewers vote to hire.
- Interviewers rate candidates as: Strong No Hire / No Hire / Leaning No Hire / Leaning Hire / Hire / Strong Hire.
- Response after onsite: typically **within 5 business days**, but can take longer for senior roles.

---

### Step 7 – Offer and Negotiation
- Offer includes: Base Salary + Sign-On Bonus + RSUs (Amazon Stock Units) + Benefits.
- Amazon has firm base salary caps (generally capped under ~$165K/₹~50 LPA for most mid-level roles globally) — this is a well-known constraint vs. Google and Meta.
- Long-term wealth at Amazon comes heavily from RSUs, not base salary.
- RSU vesting schedule: 5% in Year 1, 15% in Year 2, 40% in Year 3, 40% in Year 4 (back-loaded — unusual vs. most companies).
- Sign-on bonuses are often used to compensate for the lower Year 1 RSU payout.
- **Always negotiate** — especially on sign-on bonus and RSU grant size.
- AI and ML roles tend to receive stronger compensation packages.

## Hiring Tracks

### 1. University / New Grad Hiring (SDE I, L4)
For final/pre-final year students (0–2 years of experience). Process: OA + Phone Screen + 3–4 Onsite rounds (mostly coding + LP). No system design round typically expected. Strong focus on DSA and LP alignment.

### 2. Experienced / Lateral Hiring (SDE II and Above)
For candidates with 2+ years of experience. System Design round added from SDE II. More senior roles get multiple LP rounds and deeper system design evaluation. Process takes 4–8 weeks.

### 3. Internship Hiring (SDE Intern)
- Duration: 6–12 weeks (usually summer, May–August)
- Locations: Bengaluru, Hyderabad, Chennai (India); Seattle, NYC, SF Bay Area (USA)
- Process: OA (2 DSA + behavioral) → 1–2 Technical interviews → Offer
- Glassdoor data (423 interview reports): average hiring time for SDE intern is **59 days**
- 70% of SDE intern applicants rate the experience as positive
- Top-performing interns receive **Pre-Placement Offers (PPOs)** for SDE I full-time roles
- Real experience (Apr 2026, Glassdoor): "10 months total process, 1 coding question + behavioral questions. Coding was unconventional — required creative thinking."

## Special Hiring Programs (India-Specific)

### Amazon ML Challenge (Annual Hackathon)
- A prestigious national-level machine learning hackathon for Indian engineering students.
- **Format:** Two-stage — Online Round (72-hour hackathon with real Amazon datasets) → Grand Finale (top 10 teams present to Amazon scientists virtually).
- **Eligibility:** PhD, M.E./M.Tech, B.E./B.Tech students from all engineering campuses in India. Teams of 3–4 members.
- **Problem types:** Product classification, recommendation systems, ranking, forecasting using real Amazon catalog data.
- **Prizes:** ₹1 Lakh (Winner), ₹75K (1st Runner-Up), ₹50K (2nd Runner-Up) + certificates + goodies.
- **Career opportunity:** Top 50 teams receive **Pre-Placement Interviews (PPIs) for Applied Scientist Intern roles** at Amazon — a direct gateway to internship without standard application process.
- Real experience (Medium, Dec 2025 — Debjyoti Ray, ECE grad 2026): "My team ranked AIR 20 out of 80,000+ teams in Amazon ML Challenge 2025. Two weeks later we received the interest form for the Applied Scientist Intern role. Process: OA (2 LeetCode Medium DSA + 20 LP behavioral questions) → Technical Interviews. The LP behavioral section had 45 questions — be careful and consistent."

### Amazon ML Summer School (Annual Program)
- Launched in 2021; the **5th edition ran in 2025**.
- A 3-day intensive ML program where students learn from Amazon scientists.
- Topics: supervised learning, deep neural networks, probabilistic graphical models, dimensionality reduction, unsupervised learning, sequential models.
- **Eligibility:** B.Tech/M.Tech/PhD students graduating in 2026 or 2027 from any recognized Indian institute.
- **Selection test:** Part A — 20 MCQs on ML, probability, statistics, linear algebra. Part B — 2 Programming questions. Duration: 60 minutes.
- Not a direct hiring event but provides significant networking opportunities and resume value. Used as a pipeline for future Applied Scientist internship candidates.

### Amazon WoW (Women of the World) Program
- Amazon's career program specifically for **women in tech and other careers** at the university level.
- Offers: resume workshops, coding sessions, LP guidance, networking with Amazonian employees, virtual office tours.
- **Eligibility:** Female students (2023, 2024, 2025, 2026 batch grads) — all engineering backgrounds.
- No fixed deadline — rolling applications; apply at earliest.
- Benefits: Resume workshops, OA preparation sessions, career guidance, potential **internship and full-time offer** pathways.
- Apply at: amazonwowindia.splashthat.com

### Amazon Future Builders Program
- Amazon's learning and exposure program specifically for **first-year engineering students** in India.
- Not a direct hiring program but provides early career guidance and Amazon exposure.

### Campus Hiring (On-Campus Drives)
- Amazon visits IITs, NITs, IIITs, and select Tier-2 colleges for on-campus placement.
- Process: Pre-placement talk → Online Assessment → Technical interviews → Offer.
- Assessment conducted via HirePro for campus hires.

### Important Note on Amazon DSA Style
Unlike Google (which writes custom hard algorithmic problems), Amazon tends to ask **"practical" DSA questions** — LRU Cache, inventory systems, shopping cart data structures. Questions are largely LeetCode Easy-to-Medium, with Hard questions occasionally for SDE II+ or Bar Raiser rounds. The key differentiator at Amazon is that **behavioral LP questions appear in every round**, even coding rounds.

## System Design Topics (For Experienced Roles — SDE II and Above)

### Core Concepts to Know
- Load Balancing and Horizontal Scaling
- Caching — Redis, CDN, cache invalidation (LRU, TTL)
- SQL vs NoSQL — when to use which (Amazon-specific: know DynamoDB vs. Aurora)
- Database sharding and replication
- Microservices architecture — Amazon's "two-pizza team" model
- API design — REST, gRPC
- Message queues — Amazon SQS, Kinesis, Kafka, SNS/PubSub (know AWS-native options)
- Event-driven architecture — key Amazon pattern (decouple services via SQS/Kinesis)
- CAP Theorem — Consistency, Availability, Partition Tolerance
- CQRS — Command Query Responsibility Segregation (separate read/write paths)
- Rate Limiting
- Consistent Hashing
- Fault tolerance and high availability
- Content Delivery Networks (CDN)
- Observability — CloudWatch, distributed tracing, latency/error metrics

## Tips to Crack Amazon (2025 Updated)

- Learn all 16 Leadership Principles by heart — they appear in every single round.
- Prepare 4–5 strong STAR stories covering conflict, failure, leadership, ambiguity, and influence.
- Always use "I" not "We" — Amazon wants your individual contribution, not team achievements.
- Back every claim with data: "I reduced costs by 30%" not "I helped reduce costs."
- LRU Cache and Median from Data Stream are the two most-reported DSA questions — know them cold.
- Amazon coding problems are often "practical" (real-world inspired) vs. pure algo puzzles — practice both.
- Start with brute force, state complexity, then optimize. Always think out loud.
- For system design: start with clarifying requirements — never jump to architecture.
- Weave AWS services into your system design answers (SQS, Kinesis, DynamoDB, Aurora, CloudWatch).
- Show knowledge of Amazon's two-pizza team philosophy and event-driven microservices pattern.
- Customer Obsession is the backbone of all LPs — connect every answer back to customer impact.
- Mock behavioral interviews are as important as mock coding rounds at Amazon.
- Be honest and data-driven — Amazon is a metrics-obsessed company; vague answers fail.
- For internships: Amazon ML Challenge (top 50 teams → PPI) and Amazon WoW (women) are fast tracks.
- Treat every interviewer as a potential Bar Raiser — you never know who has veto power.
- Do not panic at the Bar Raiser round — it may feel more like a conversation than a traditional interview.

## Difficulty Level

**High** — Amazon is consistently ranked among the most difficult companies to crack, though slightly more accessible than Google for DSA due to the higher weight of behavioral questions. The Bar Raiser system makes the process uniquely unpredictable — a technically strong candidate can be rejected on LP grounds. Conversely, a technically average candidate can succeed with outstanding LP demonstrations. As of 2025, the behavioral bar has been raised significantly. With the 5-day RTO policy, culture fit (LP alignment) has become even more important to Amazon.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Does Amazon hire freshers from non-IIT colleges?**
A: Yes. Amazon hires from all colleges if the candidate clears the OA and interviews. Strong DSA skills, competitive programming profiles, GitHub projects, and hackathon wins (especially ML Challenge) can compensate for college brand.

**Q: What is the Bar Raiser and why does it matter?**
A: The Bar Raiser is a specially trained Amazon interviewer from outside your team. They have veto power over the hiring decision. Even if all other interviewers say "hire," the Bar Raiser can block the offer. They evaluate whether you raise the overall talent bar at Amazon.

**Q: How many total rounds are there?**
A: Typically 6–8 rounds total: OA + Phone Screen + 3–4 Onsite rounds (including Bar Raiser). New grads usually face 3–4 rounds in onsite. Experienced candidates face 5–7 rounds.

**Q: Can I reapply if rejected?**
A: Yes. Amazon typically enforces a 6–12 month cooldown period before reapplication for the same role. First-attempt rejection does not permanently close the door.

**Q: Is system design asked for SDE I (new grad)?**
A: Generally no. SDE I onsite typically consists of coding/DSA rounds and LP rounds. Some roles may have a very basic OOD (Object-Oriented Design) problem like "Design a parking lot" — but this is lightweight compared to SDE II+ system design.

**Q: What is the RSU vesting schedule at Amazon?**
A: 5% in Year 1, 15% in Year 2, 40% in Year 3, 40% in Year 4. This back-loaded structure means Year 1 take-home can feel low; sign-on bonuses are often structured to offset this.

**Q: Does Amazon offer remote roles in India?**
A: Limited, due to the 2025 return-to-office mandate. Check individual job listings on amazon.jobs. Some specific teams/roles may still offer hybrid arrangements.

**Q: How important is CGPA for Amazon?**
A: A CGPA of 7.0+ is typically required/preferred for freshers at the application stage. However, real-world project experience, strong coding skills, and competitive programming profiles can compensate for average CGPA.

**Q: What is the Amazon WoW Program?**
A: Amazon Women of the World — a career program for female engineering students offering workshops, networking, and a direct pathway to internship and full-time offers. Open to 2023–2026 batch female grads across all colleges.
